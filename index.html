Wave-Particle Duality in Crystalline Structures

🌊

de Broglie Hypothesis

Louis de Broglie (1924) proposed every moving particle has an associated wavelength, confirming wave-particle duality:

λ = h / p = h / √(2meV)
h = 6.626 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s (Planck's constant) m = 9.109 × 10⁻³¹ kg (electron mass) e = 1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ C (electron charge) V = accelerating voltage
💎

Bragg's Law

W.L. Bragg (1913) — constructive interference occurs when path difference equals integer multiple of wavelength:

nλ = 2d · sin(θ)
n = diffraction order (1,2,3...) d = inter-planar spacing (Å) θ = Bragg glancing angle
⚡

Davisson-Germer Experiment

In 1927, Davisson & Germer confirmed electron wave behavior by observing diffraction from a Ni crystal, earning the Nobel Prize:

Peak at φ = 50°, V = 54V, d = 2.15 Å
λ (measured) = 1.65 Å λ (de Broglie) = 1.67 Å ✓

Relativistic Correction

At voltages above ~100 kV, electrons reach relativistic speeds, requiring a corrected wavelength formula:

λᵣ = h / √(2m₀eV · (1 + eV/2m₀c²))
At 100kV: classical 3.88 pm → relativistic 3.70 pm At 200kV: classical 2.74 pm → relativistic 2.51 pm
